## 1. A machine, not an intelligence: how Chinese programmers imagine generative AI (2026)

- Journal: CSR / Chinese Sociological Review
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1080/21620555.2026.2620115

**Research question**: How programmers, as intermediaries between generative AI and society, imagine this technology.

**Data / material**: survey data; platform/social media data; text corpus

**Method**: computational text analysis; mixed methods

**Key findings**: Two imaginaries in online discussions—"AI-as-Machine" (controllable tool) and "AI-as-Intelligence" (autonomous agent). Survey confirms dominance of "AI-as-Machine" among technically proficient programmers.

## 2. Computational Basis of LLMs' Decision Making in Social Simulation (2026)

- Journal: SOCIOL METHODOL / Sociological Methodology

**Research question**: Methods for probing, quantifying, and modifying an LLM's internal representations in a dictator game.

**Method**: experiment

**Key findings**: Extracting "vectors of variable variations" from LLM internal state; manipulating these can alter how variables relate to decision making. Implications for alignment, debiasing, and social simulations.
